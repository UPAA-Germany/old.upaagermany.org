2015 November 18-20, 25-27 - The First Reel: Philippine Film Festival - Berlin
Kino Moviemento, Kottbusser Damm 22, Kreuzberg, Berlin
The First Reel was a non-profit festival ran by volunteers such as Trinka Lat and Lola Abrera that aimed to support Filipino filmmakers. It was collaborated with the Philippine Studies Series Berlin (of Rosa Castillo, a UPAA-Germany member) and the Philippine Embassy in Germany. Shown at the film festival were Filipino feature-length films and innovative shorts; as well as Roundtable Discussions that tackled topics on identity, Southeast Asian cinema, and visual storytelling; and Filipino food courtesy of AYAN - Filipino Streetfood, Cusina Pilipina, and PINOY Philippinische Spezialitäten.
Special guests: Ricky Davao of Boses and Jay Bordon of Ang Huling Cha-Cha ni Anita 
Closing night performance: Singer and songwriter Mishka Adams (@mishkaadamsmusic)

2015 Sept. 12 (Saturday, 2pm) Free screening of Hannah Espia's directorial debut film "Transit"
Kaiser-Friedrichstr. 33, 10627 Berlin 
Transit is a 2013 Filipino independent drama film written and directed by Hannah Espia. The film follows a story about a single father who is forced to hide his children from immigration police in Israel after the Israeli government decides to deport children of immigrant workers. [1] It is Espia's full-length debut film. It was mostly shot in Israel. The film competed under the New Breed section of Cinemalaya 2013. The film won Best Film, directing, acting and other technical awards. Wikipedia

﻿2015 Sept. 5 (Saturday, 5pm - 6pm) - UPAA Germany e.V. presents Vincent Valiente on his First Vocal Recital 
A Vocal Recital by Vincent Valiente, tenor, with Kazuhiro Shigematsu, piano, was presented by the association at the Gemeindesaal der Heilig-Geist-Kirche in Berlin last September 5 at 5pm. The recital had two parts: European classical music from the 16th century to the 19th century by Caccini, Gluck, Bellini, Handel, and Mozart; and Philippine classical music in the last two centuries by Zubiri, Santiago, de Guzman, and Fuentes. 
After the recital, a farewell party was held for Vincent Valiente who will study for a year at the University of Edinburgh.

2015 June 24 (Wednesday, 7pm - 10:30pm) - DAYAW. Filipino-inspired POP-up kitchen
Markthalle Neun, Eisenbahnstrasse 42/43 - Pücklerstrasse 34, 10997 Berlin
The tickets to the launching of the Dayaw Filipino-inspired Pop-up Kitchen was sold-out the night just before the event. There were around 120 persons including the cooks, the volunteers/usherettes, the musicians, the journalists including those from ABS-CBN, and our very own Ambassador Melita Sta.Maria-Thomeczek with some other diplomats from the Philippine Embassy Berlin. 
It was a successful meal/performance designed by Pepe Dayaw, curated by Kathrin Kuna in partnership with the Markthalle Neun and in cooperation with the Philippine Embassy - Berlin with the volunteer participation of the University of the Philippines Alumni Association e.V. 
The menu included kare-kare, kinilaw, pisbol (fishball), gazpacho, sinigang, adobo-kamatis-atsara, champorado topped with crispy anchovy tempura, and puto bumbong. 
The event was also lined up with performances from Filipino musicians - singer-songwriters Mishka Adams and Isa Tabasuares, couple Jomar and Myls Rubio - and a brazilian singer Danny. Filipino songs like Ugoy ng Duyan were sung. As a closing, everyone sang Bato sa Buhangin, with lyrics distributed to the guests.

2015 June 6 (Saturday, 2pm - 5pm) -  Wohnzimmer Lecture - The kutiyapi and other boat lutes in the Philippines, part 1
The "kudyapì" is considered the national musical instrument of the Philippines. However, there is not much known about the great variety of different shapes and designs as well as different styles of boat lute music, each of them typical for specific ethnic groups in the Philippines. This Philippine Studies Series Berlin lecture which is co-organised by the University of the Philippines Alumni Association Germany presented video samples of boat lute playing from many indigenous peoples cultures in Mindanao (Magindanaon, Talaandig, Tboli, Blaan, Tasaday, Tagakaulu, Agusan Manobo, Ata, Matigsalug, Obo, Dulangan and Western Bukidnon Manobo, Mandaya, Mangguangan and Subanen) as well as Palawan (Pala’wan and Batak).

The wohnzimmer discussion was given by Hans Brandeis, a German ethnomusicologist. During 15 field trips, starting in 1976 and comprising a period of four years, he collected data on Philippine traditional music. In the 1980s, he mainly studied the music of the Bukidnon (Talaandig, Higaonon) and since the 1990s the boat lute traditions (kutiyapì et al.) in the Philippines and other parts of Southeast Asia. He premiered part 1 of his film on boat lutes during the discussion.

2015 April 25 (Saturday, 2pm - 4pm) - The BalikPhD Roadshow in Berlin

Dr. Gisela Padilla-Concepcion, Vice President for Academic Affairs of the University of the Philippines (UP) System, delivered a presentation at the Philippine Embassy in Berlin on the current programs of UP encouraging foreign- trained Filipinos to be part of the internationalisation and enhancement of   UP’s   educational   and   research activities. Academicians and other interested participants from different parts of Germany attended the BalikPhD seminar which was co-hosted by the University of the Philippines Alumni Association Germany e.V. (UPAA Germany), The Filipino Students, Alumni and Academics in Germany (FSAAG) and the Philippine Embassy in Berlin.

The BalikPhD program which offers relocation package and start-up research grant for balikbayan experts has been discussed in the seminar. UP is inviting applicants from all fields, especially those who can serve best the interests of the nation towards nation-building.
